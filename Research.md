# Research

To build a map with a coherent sense of place, I conducted research across four areas: real-world architectural reference, in-game visual reference, WAD feasibility, and written sources on level design. The goal was to ground the theme in something specific and buildable.

---

## Key Reference: Narrow Calle with Arch

![x](https://drive.google.com/file/d/1CidHrVmsVXZCCZMYwuhyP92Az51cWGwB/view?usp=drive_link)

This image is the most important single reference for the map's core spatial unit.

A narrow passage: approximately 1.5 to 2 metres wide: runs between two building masses. The walls rise four to five storeys. The strip of sky overhead is thin. Partway down, an arch connects the buildings above, creating a covered section. A single figure is visible walking away, giving clear human scale. The floor is flat flagstone.

**What this gives the map:**

*Scale.* The passage is just wide enough for the player to move through without feeling like a corridor, but tight enough that an enemy at the far end is a real threat. There is no room to strafe meaningfully. Quake's player capsule width is the reference: the calle should feel like it was built for people, not for combat.

*The arch as choke point.* The overhead arch divides the passage into two zones: before and after. Enemies can position themselves in either zone. The player cannot see through to the far side until they are committed. This is the primary tension mechanic of the calle sections.

*Vertical scale.* The building height relative to the passage width creates a strong sense of enclosure. In Quake geometry, tall walls with a narrow sky gap above communicate this even without detailed facade work. The sky texture should be visible but only as a strip: never an open expanse.

*Texture language.* The left wall is bare plaster, stained and peeling. The right wall is smooth and yellow. The floor is grey flagstone. In WAD terms: a rough plaster texture for upper walls, a smoother lighter texture for the facing wall, and a dark stone tile for the floor. The variety across a single short passage reads as age and irregular maintenance rather than designed decoration.

*Orientation.* The passage reads as a connector: it goes somewhere. The figure walking away reinforces this. The map uses this geometry exclusively as connectors between the larger spaces (campo, canal bridge, courtyard), never as destinations in themselves.

## WAD Research

The map needs to feel like Venice without becoming generic medieval fantasy. WAD selection is the primary tool for this.

**id1 (Quake retail textures)**
Strong rough stonework and dark interiors. Good for foundations, crypts, and the lower canal-level areas. The palette is very dark: needs supplementing for exterior surfaces.

**sock_medieval / InfernalFortress**
High-detail Gothic stonework, pointed arch trim textures, good decorative border tiles. The arch trim textures are directly usable for the sottoportego ceiling geometry. The floor tiles work for the piano nobile interior rooms.

**Arcane Dimensions (ad)**
Large-format stone tiles, good plaster-equivalent wall surfaces in warm ochre and cream tones, iron grating textures. The ochre wall surfaces are the closest available match to Venetian plaster facades. The grating textures work for canal-level water gate openings.

**Custom / supplemental**
A small set of custom textures may be needed for the canal water surface and the sky. The water texture needs to read as dark and opaque: not the standard Quake slime or water. A near-black animated texture with subtle surface movement is the target.

# Areas and Bubble Diagram

Once the theme was established through research, the next step was breaking the map into distinct areas. Each area needs a reason to exist: a narrative purpose that explains what the space is, and a gameplay purpose that defines what the player does there. The areas also need to work together as a sequence: pacing, variety, and the isolation principle all depend on how they connect.

The area names follow the same logic as the rooms in Resident Evil 2's police station. The name implies a place that had a life before the player arrived, and that history is readable in the space itself.

---

## Area 1: The Canal Gate

### Narrative

The Canal Gate is the map's entry point: a water gate (androne) at the base of a large palazzo, opening directly onto the canal. Boats once moored here. Iron rings are bolted into the stone below the waterline. The gate itself hangs open, one hinge broken, the wood swollen and dark with damp.

- **Where is this place?** The outer edge of the district, facing the lagoon. The first thing you see when you arrive by water.
- **What happened here recently?** The gate was forced open from the outside. Something came in this way.
- **What should the player look for?** The waterline on the walls: it is higher than it should be. The canal level has risen since the invasion.
- **What rooms does it normally contain?** The androne (ground-floor boat hall), a mooring area, a stair up to the piano nobile, a small storage room with rope and tackle.
- **How do people interact with it?** Deliveries arrived here. Gondoliers waited in the androne. The stairs led up to the merchant who owned the building.
- **How does it connect?** The androne opens onto the fondamenta (canal-side walkway) and the first calle heading into the district.
- **Architectural style:** Lower Venetian Gothic. Rough rusticated stonework at the base, water-stained plaster above. Round arch over the water entrance.

### Gameplay

Start of level. No enemies. The player enters through the water gate and emerges onto the fondamenta. This is the tutorial space: the player learns that the canal water is hazardous (they can see it close-up, can fall in, take damage, and climb out), and that the map is built around water crossings. The fondamenta runs left to a dead end (establishing that the map opens rightward) and right toward the first bridge. A single Grunt is visible on the far side of the bridge: the first enemy, visible before the player reaches combat range. No combat until the bridge crossing.

## Area 2: The Campo dei Morti

### Narrative

The campo is the district's central open square. Its name: the Field of the Dead: predates the invasion; it was named for a plague cemetery that occupied the site centuries before the buildings went up. The campo is now the hub of the Quake occupation. Enemies patrol here between other assignments.

- **Where is this place?** The geographic centre of the district. Every major building faces onto it.
- **What happened here recently?** The campo was the site of the human defenders' last organised resistance. A barricade was built across the north end. It was pushed aside and burned. Scorch marks remain on the paving stones.
- **What is about to happen?** The enemies in the campo are responding to the player's arrival: patrol routes are tighter than they were, converging toward the church.
- **What should the player look for?** The campanile. Its bell still rings occasionally, with no visible mechanism. It serves as the map's primary landmark and can be seen from most other areas.
- **Who's really in control?** Something in the church behind the campo. The enemies patrol toward it, not away from it.
- **What rooms does it normally contain?** Open paving, a central stone wellhead, market stalls along the south side (now overturned), the facades of three major buildings, the church entrance on the north end.
- **Architectural style:** Open campo paving in Istrian stone, surrounding facades in ochre and red plaster, Gothic windows above, the church's white marble Baroque facade dominating the north.

### Gameplay

Hub area. The campo is the map's orientation space: the player arrives here from the Canal Gate bridge and can see multiple exits. Three combat encounters, introduced sequentially as the player explores the campo's perimeter:

1. Two Grunts on patrol in the open campo. Long range, open ground: the player has room to move.
2. An Ogre in the ruined market stall area on the south side. Tighter space, grenade threat.
3. A Grunt on the church steps, guarding the entrance to Area 3.

After clearing the campo, the player has three visible routes: the church entrance (Area 3), a calle heading east (Area 4), and a locked courtyard gate to the west. The locked gate creates a question the player will later answer.

## Area 3: The Palazzo del Consiglio

### Narrative

The Palazzo del Consiglio was the district's administrative building: the seat of the local governing council. It occupies the full north side of the campo, adjacent to the church. Its piano nobile contains the council chamber, a record room, and the private apartments of the head councillor. The building is the most intact structure in the district. The Quake forces have not damaged it because something inside is useful to them.

- **What happened here recently?** The council records were searched. Drawers are open, papers on the floor. Whatever was found has been removed.
- **What here is powerful or valuable?** The silver key to the crypt beneath the church. The council held it as a civic trust. It is now somewhere in this building.
- **What here is not what it appears to be?** The councillor's private study has a second door behind a bookcase. This was how the council secretly communicated with the church.
- **What rooms does it normally contain?** Grand entrance hall, council chamber with a long table, record room, private study, servant stairs, rooftop loggia.
- **Architectural style:** Venetian Gothic piano nobile. Ornate window tracery facing the campo, gilded ceiling beams in the council chamber, marble floors, tapestries (now torn).

### Gameplay

Exploration and secrets area. One combat encounter on entry (two Grunts in the entrance hall). The rest of the building is a non-combat space for exploration and puzzle-solving. The player searches for the silver key. Finding it requires reading the environment: the record room has been searched, so the key isn't there; the private study's hidden door leads to a small passage containing the key.

The rooftop loggia provides a vista over the campo and a sight line to the sottoportego entrance (Area 4) below: the player can see where they need to go next before they get there. The loggia also connects via a narrow walkway to the church roof, which is a secret route into Area 5.

## Area 4: The Sottoportego dell'Acqua

### Narrative

The Sottoportego dell'Acqua runs beneath three building masses between the campo and the outer canal district. It is the oldest passage in the area: its stone ceiling predates the surrounding buildings by two centuries. The name refers to an aqueduct channel that once ran above it. The channel is now breached and the passage floods at the far end.

- **What happened here recently?** Something moved through here from the direction of the canal: the mud on the floor shows marks heading inward, not outward.
- **What should the player look for?** A side door partway through, iron-banded, locked from the other side. It can be heard but not opened from within the passage. It leads to the flooded crypt (Area 5) and can only be accessed from that direction.
- **What here is not what it appears to be?** The passage appears to dead-end at the flooded section. The floor of the flooded section has an opening: a cistern entrance: that leads down and forward.
- **What rooms does it normally contain?** A single long passage, approximately 40 metres, with a ceiling height of 2.2 metres. A small alcove partway through (formerly a watchman's post). The flooded far section.
- **Architectural style:** Raw stone, no plaster, wooden beam ceiling at the alcove, flat stone arch throughout. The oldest visible stonework in the map.

### Gameplay

Terror zone. The player enters from the campo-adjacent calle. The passage is dark: one light source at the entry arch, one at the far end, nothing between. No room to strafe. Two encounters:

1. Entry: A Grunt positioned in the alcove partway through. The player cannot see them until they are within 10 units of the alcove. Close range, forced engagement.
2. Far end: A Vore waiting in the flooded section. The confined space makes projectile avoidance extremely difficult. The player must push through quickly or take significant damage.

The cistern entrance at the flooded section is the exit: the player drops down into Area 5. There is no return through the sottoportego. Path closes behind.

## Area 5: The Flooded Crypt

### Narrative

The crypt beneath the church is the oldest structure on the site: older than the church above it, older than the district. The church was built over it deliberately, as a form of containment. The crypt has been partially flooded for centuries. The current water level is higher than historical records suggest it should be.

- **What is about to happen?** The player's arrival is the final trigger. The entity sealed here is awake. This is the climax of the map.
- **What here is powerful or valuable?** The sealed chamber at the far end. The entity itself.
- **What here is not what it appears to be?** The stone sarcophagi in the crypt are not burial containers. They are components of the sealing mechanism. Destroying them weakens the entity.
- **Who's really in control?** The entity. It has been in control of the district since before the invasion. The Quake forces arrived because it called them.
- **What rooms does it normally contain?** A flooded antechamber with columns, a nave-like central hall, side chapels (now submerged to window height), the sealed inner chamber.
- **Architectural style:** Pre-Gothic Romanesque. Round arches, heavy undressed stone, no ornamentation. The walls are covered in a faint bioluminescent growth near the waterline.

### Gameplay

Climax. The player arrives via the cistern drop from Area 4. The crypt is partially flooded: navigable but the water causes slow damage, forcing movement. Three sequential encounters building to the boss:

1. Antechamber: Four Grunts and two Ogres. The columns provide cover but the water slows movement. Introduction to the flooded combat dynamic.
2. Central hall: Two Knights and a Vore. The Vore positions on a raised stone platform above the waterline. The player must choose between staying in cover (in the water) or taking the platform (exposed to the Knights).
3. Inner chamber: Boss encounter. The sealed chamber opens when the player uses the silver key from Area 3. The boss is a Shambler. Destroying the three sarcophagi (shootable triggers) weakens it progressively. The exit: the level end trigger: is visible through a grate on the far wall from the moment the player enters the chamber, unreachable until the Shambler is dead.

## Bubble Diagram

The bubble diagram below shows how these five areas connect and the overall flow of the map. The main path runs left to right. Secondary connections (shown as dashed lines) allow the player to loop back or access areas through alternate routes.

![Bubble Diagram](https://drive.google.com/file/d/1d4uLUZhMnEVjQNNaOiCidfBuyHbnuZqP/view?usp=drive_link)

**Notes on flow:**

- **The Campo dei Morti is the hub.** The player passes through it twice: once on the way in, once after clearing the Palazzo. This creates a sense of the city as a real place with multiple routes, and the second visit feels different because the player now knows the space.
- **The Sottoportego is a one-way valve.** Once the player enters, there is no return. This is the map's point of no return and the transition from the upper city to the underground.
- **The Palazzo loggia creates a view ahead.** The player can see the sottoportego entrance from the rooftop before they go there. This is the map's primary use of views and vistas as a layout tool.
- **The locked courtyard gate in the Campo is a false lead.** It can be opened from the Palazzo side (a secondary connection), but it only loops back to the canal fondamenta: a reward for exploration, not a shortcut to progression.
- **The silver key from Area 3 is required for Area 5.** This creates a hard dependency between the Palazzo and the Crypt, ensuring the player experiences Area 3 before the climax even if they find a shortcut into the crypt.
